Buying a Home

Are you considering buying a home and have a pre-qualification letter from your lender? Now the fun begins and you get to visit open homes with true purpose. Choosing a place to call home can be based on a price range, commute to work, proximity to family or a school district. My latest home was based on a combination of the style of home distance from work, so the home has a smaller kitchen and a 10 minute commute to work.

After you’ve selected your first and second choices, you work with a buying agent to determine your bid price. There is an intrinsic element of how important it is for you to secure that home. Your agent, the buyer’s agent, is your advocate and provides consultation throughout the transaction. The seller’s agent should have the home owner’s best interest in mind. Although California does allow an agent to represent both the buyer and the seller when both parties agree to the full disclosure, it is recommended to have two different agents represent each side, so that there is negotiating power for both the buyer and seller. This is one of the main reasons why I signed on with Keller Williams because it has a network of over 167,00 realtors and agents worldwide as of 2017.

When I purchased my first condo many, many years ago, I didn’t have a buyer’s agent represent me. I was young and naive and it didn’t appear the agent had my best interest in mind because she was hyper focused on her commission. When I purchased my next home, I signed on with a buyer’s agent to represent me and it made a world of difference. She advised me on what I needed to do to secure my mortgage, improvements that could be made on the home and the bid amount based on market trends. Partnering with a buyer’s agent was a wonderful experience.

Bidding on a home has no guarantee the seller will accept the offer. Surprisingly, not all buyers will accept the highest bid. Their agent may point out risk factors such as a buyer not being able to secure a mortgage, but a second bid came in slightly lower from an all cash buyer. What is a cash buyer you may ask? This is a buyer that has the ability to buy a home with cash and does not have to rely on a bank to borrow money to purchase a home. Even though a buyer offers a higher bid, the seller may be informed the bank will need a couple of months for the underwriter to prepare the mortgage. When bidding on a home, the seller and the seller’s agent should feel confident the offer they accept will close successfully.

Appraisals are based on comparable homes that have recently sold. The appraisers are professionals who determine the fair market value for a home based on the square footage, number of bedrooms, bathrooms, condition of the home and other factors. They determine the value added from recent remodels or architectural additions to the home. The appraiser does not take an average of the recently sold homes because each home may be slightly different. Even though condos or subdivisions have identical floor plans and square footage, the countertops and flooring could have been upgraded by the previous owners. One side of a complex could have a scenic view, which can also add value to a home.

Escrow is a neutral third party that is that keeps the funds and distributes the funds after the title is transferred. There are many financial elements that are divided between various parties on both the buying and selling side and the escrow company ensures the funds are allocated correctly. A downpayment can be submitted to the escrow company for safekeeping. It also works with the bank and insurance companies to ensure everything needed to finalize the transaction is received.

When your contracted closing date is approaching, your agent will meet with you to provide a final walk through just to make sure any necessary repairs were completed or the home was in the same condition as it was at the time your offer was accepted. Each home purchase is slightly different even though there are similar elements, so this brief summary doesn’t capture every scenario that can delay a purchase. The agents work with the clients and third parties to make all of the necessary corrections.

You will meet with the escrow or title company to sign all of the final paperwork that summarizes all of the steps, agreed upon price, payments and other details relating to the transfer of title. Congratulations! You’ve made it and you get to receive the keys to your new home.

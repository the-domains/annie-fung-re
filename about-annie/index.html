About Annie

Starting my journey…

At the tender age of 10, I used to accompany my grandmother on her monthly rounds to collect rent which introduced me to real estate at a very young age. The most valuable advice she had given me was that possessing the ability to navigate the complex regulations of real estate wasn’t more important than building long term friendships. An acquaintance may become a client, tenant or business partner and over the years they become friends.

Exploring the Bay Area

My grandmother’s involvement with real estate and property management piqued my mother’s interest, so she took me to open houses throughout the Bay Area for several years. Stepping into a circa 1900-1920 home resembled time travel. Bay Area homes' architectural details varied and had its own unique characteristics such as coffered ceilings, wainscoting, base and crown molding, or modern minimalism. Entering a condo or subdivision home that has been updated was always a pleasant surprise for me because a little bit of the previous owners’ personality shined through. The previous owner’s creative design element is gifted to the new resident.

Learning about architecture

Being a native San Franciscan meant I grew up in a city that has a high walkability score and I’ve trekked the roughly 7 by 7 miles or 46.87 square miles to explore the different architectural styles of homes in the city. San Francisco is famously known for its quintessential Victorian painted ladies on Steiner Street, but each neighborhood has its own character. One can find a Tudor, mission hacienda, mid-century modern, Spanish, art deco, high-rise, and so many other types of homes in the city. Nestled in Maiden Lane is the V.C. Morris Gift Ship designed by Frank Lloyd Wright the “father of modern architect.”

As a teenager, I used to assist an Interiors Design decorator and spent many weekends visiting hosts’ homes throughout the bay area. We traveled as far north as Santa Rosa and as far south as the suburbs of San Leandro. One family’s home was redecorated in 1960, but everything was still pristine and survived decades of evolving trends. There was earth-orange colored fluffy shag carpeting in the living room, a medium wood toned dining room set, and an avocado green fridge in the kitchen. The home itself could have been a time capsule, and each guest that attended the Interiors Design event had a beaming smile while they noticed how well preserved everything was. A professionally staged home can have a similar affect and can leave a positive and very lasting impression.

Searching for my first apartment brought me to the peninsula, and I have lived in San Carlos and San Mateo for a few years. The Hillside Apartments in San Mateo are nearly identical to the Park Merced Apartments in San Francisco and a cab driver had informed me there are also identical apartments in New York. Leonard Schultze was the architect who designed these large scale apartment complexes, and this style of apartment can be found in the Los Angeles area as well.

My childhood friend was searching for a new home, and she had introduced me to the concept of an Eichler home. A Joseph Leopold Eichler developed home has a modern exterior with clean lines, and the interior has an open core surrounded by glass walls and a full view of the yard. There is an Eichler community in San Mateo County, and a few Eichler style homes can be found in the South Bay.

Craftsman style bungalows brought me to the East Bay and the dark wood trim and ceiling beams with contrasting bright white walls is beautiful. A licensed contractor had explained to me that older homes are built with old growth redwood that has more tannins, which naturally makes it more rot resistant and can last for centuries when cared for properly. In the past, these Craftsman styled bungalows were sold as kits from Sears. This popular architectural style can be found throughout the states.

During spring when the cherry blossom lined streets of San Ramon were in full bloom, I had visited an open house. It had rained just a few days before, and I recall seeing the breathtaking rolling green hills of the Tri-Valley area and decided to live there for a few years. Dublin was an up and coming city at the time and there were more new homes than I was accustomed to seeing. These luxurious homes had high ceilings, central air conditioning, double paned windows and were pre-wired with Ethernet ports.

The South Bay is where I currently call home and there is a vast array of different styled homes and eclectic neighborhoods in this area. People make a house a home and the beauty of the Bay Area is that each region has its own character. Each neighborhood has its own favorite bakery, grocery store, or gathering area that brings people together. I am still exploring San Jose’s 180 square miles of city.

Journey to becoming a real estate agent Lic# 02048903

Exposure to real estate at a young age and having been a client as a buyer and seller naturally lead me to becoming a real estate agent. Several decades ago, I worked at a start up named Owners.com that specialized in the for sale by owner (FSBO) market. When I sold my first home, I had decided to use a real estate agent professional because I understood how complex and time consuming selling a home could be. The home sold at the first showing and the sales transaction went smoothly. Had I not used the realtor’s services, I don’t believe the home would have sold as quickly. Over the decades as a client I have worked with other realtors, and they made each home purchase and sale a success because they were the experts in their profession. I was inspired to become a real estate agent because the searching, buying, maintaining and selling of my home homes was such a great experience I wanted to be able to provide guidance to my potential clients.

I have empathy for young adults who aspire to become homeowners one day because I too was once a young adult who had college debt, had faced economic turmoil and was pummeled by the great recession, but I reassure you home ownership is not impossible. The pinnacle moment for first time buyers is when they finally receive the keys to their new home.

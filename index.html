Living in the Bay Area and Silicon Valley

California is a beautiful coastal state and there is so much to do and there are so many places to visit. The urban areas of the Bay Area and Silicon Valley are a short drive to nature parks and beaches. The cities offer fine dining, culture, performing arts and theater.

Ask a Question. Wanting to Relocate?

The SAP event center in San Jose, CA is the home for the Sharks ice hockey team. Several concerts are held at the SAP each year. The arena is located near downtown San Jose and is walking distance to several restaurants and San Pedro Square Market. Santana Row is a short drive away from downtown San Jose. During the day there is shopping and the nightlife starts after the sun sets.

Famers’ markets are very popular and one can be found on almost every day of the week in different parts of the Bay Area. Saturday Farmers’ Markets are held on in San Francisco, Palo Alto, Saratoga and Pleasanton. There’s a Farmers’ Market in Campbell and Cupertino on Sundays. San Carlos has a Farmers’ Market every Thursday until the start of the winter season.

Golden Gate Park, Muir Woods, the Bay Trail, Rancho San Antonio and Los Gatos Creek Trail name only a few parks and nature trails in the Bay Area. The Tech Museum, San Francisco Academy of Science, De Young Museum, Legion of Honor, and the San Francisco Museum of Modern Art list a few museums. There are also botanical gardens in San Jose, Berkeley, San Francisco and the Filoli House in Woodside.

Selling Your Home

Deciding to sell your home is a big decision and each reason is slightly different. Some individuals need to relocate for their careers, families outgrow their current home and others may want to downsize. The ebb and flow of the exchange of ownership is natural and real estate agents are realtors are available to provide guidance, industry advice and consultation. Agents are available to assist you when you are ready to list your home.

The strategy in pricing a home is based on the market, supply and demand. At the end of the day, the home is worth what the buyer is willing to pay. The seller and buyer’s agent will draft the contract for signature. A seller can counter an offer and the buyer can accept or counter. A seller doesn't have to counter and can accept the initial offer. The agents’ goal is to protect their clients’ best interest.

Your agent can also represent you as a buying agent if you are needing to purchase a different home. If you are moving out of state, it is recommended to work with an agent local to that area. My California Real Estate license allows me to provide service to the Bay Area.

When you are thinking about selling your home or want to have a conversation about real estate, please contact me at afung@kw.com. I may be able to answer any questions you may have.

About Annie

Starting my journey…

At the tender age of 10, I used to accompany my grandmother on her monthly rounds to collect rent which introduced me to real estate at a very young age. The most valuable advice she had given me was that possessing the ability to navigate the complex regulations of real estate wasn’t more important than building long term friendships. An acquaintance may become a client, tenant or business partner and over the years they become friends.

Exploring the Bay Area

My grandmother’s involvement with real estate and property management piqued my mother’s interest, so she took me to open houses throughout the Bay Area for several years. Stepping into a circa 1900-1920 home resembled time travel. Bay Area homes' architectural details varied and had its own unique characteristics such as coffered ceilings, wainscoting, base and crown molding, or modern minimalism. Entering a condo or subdivision home that has been updated was always a pleasant surprise for me because a little bit of the previous owners’ personality shined through. The previous owner’s creative design element is gifted to the new resident.

Learning about architecture

Being a native San Franciscan meant I grew up in a city that has a high walkability score and I’ve trekked the roughly 7 by 7 miles or 46.87 square miles to explore the different architectural styles of homes in the city. San Francisco is famously known for its quintessential Victorian painted ladies on Steiner Street, but each neighborhood has its own character. One can find a Tudor, mission hacienda, mid-century modern, Spanish, art deco, high-rise, and so many other types of homes in the city. Nestled in Maiden Lane is the V.C. Morris Gift Ship designed by Frank Lloyd Wright the “father of modern architect.”

As a teenager, I used to assist an Interiors Design decorator and spent many weekends visiting hosts’ homes throughout the bay area. We traveled as far north as Santa Rosa and as far south as the suburbs of San Leandro. One family’s home was redecorated in 1960, but everything was still pristine and survived decades of evolving trends. There was earth-orange colored fluffy shag carpeting in the living room, a medium wood toned dining room set, and an avocado green fridge in the kitchen. The home itself could have been a time capsule, and each guest that attended the Interiors Design event had a beaming smile while they noticed how well preserved everything was. A professionally staged home can have a similar affect and can leave a positive and very lasting impression.

Searching for my first apartment brought me to the peninsula, and I have lived in San Carlos and San Mateo for a few years. The Hillside Apartments in San Mateo are nearly identical to the Park Merced Apartments in San Francisco and a cab driver had informed me there are also identical apartments in New York. Leonard Schultze was the architect who designed these large scale apartment complexes, and this style of apartment can be found in the Los Angeles area as well.

My childhood friend was searching for a new home, and she had introduced me to the concept of an Eichler home. A Joseph Leopold Eichler developed home has a modern exterior with clean lines, and the interior has an open core surrounded by glass walls and a full view of the yard. There is an Eichler community in San Mateo County, and a few Eichler style homes can be found in the South Bay.

Craftsman style bungalows brought me to the East Bay and the dark wood trim and ceiling beams with contrasting bright white walls is beautiful. A licensed contractor had explained to me that older homes are built with old growth redwood that has more tannins, which naturally makes it more rot resistant and can last for centuries when cared for properly. In the past, these Craftsman styled bungalows were sold as kits from Sears. This popular architectural style can be found throughout the states.

During spring when the cherry blossom lined streets of San Ramon were in full bloom, I had visited an open house. It had rained just a few days before, and I recall seeing the breathtaking rolling green hills of the Tri-Valley area and decided to live there for a few years. Dublin was an up and coming city at the time and there were more new homes than I was accustomed to seeing. These luxurious homes had high ceilings, central air conditioning, double paned windows and were pre-wired with Ethernet ports.

The South Bay is where I currently call home and there is a vast array of different styled homes and eclectic neighborhoods in this area. People make a house a home and the beauty of the Bay Area is that each region has its own character. Each neighborhood has its own favorite bakery, grocery store, or gathering area that brings people together. I am still exploring San Jose’s 180 square miles of city.

Buying a Home

Are you considering buying a home and have a pre-qualification letter from your lender? Now the fun begins and you get to visit open homes with true purpose. Choosing a place to call home can be based on a price range, commute to work, proximity to family or a school district. My latest home was based on a combination of the style of home distance from work, so the home has a smaller kitchen and a 10 minute commute to work.

After you’ve selected your first and second choices, you work with a buying agent to determine your bid price. There is an intrinsic element of how important it is for you to secure that home. Your agent, the buyer’s agent, is your advocate and provides consultation throughout the transaction. The seller’s agent should have the home owner’s best interest in mind. Although California does allow an agent to represent both the buyer and the seller when both parties agree to the full disclosure, it is recommended to have two different agents represent each side, so that there is negotiating power for both the buyer and seller. This is one of the main reasons why I signed on with Keller Williams because it has a network of over 167,00 realtors and agents worldwide as of 2017.

When I purchased my first condo many, many years ago, I didn’t have a buyer’s agent represent me. I was young and naive and it didn’t appear the agent had my best interest in mind because she was hyper focused on her commission. When I purchased my next home, I signed on with a buyer’s agent to represent me and it made a world of difference. She advised me on what I needed to do to secure my mortgage, improvements that could be made on the home and the bid amount based on market trends. Partnering with a buyer’s agent was a wonderful experience.

Bidding on a home has no guarantee the seller will accept the offer. Surprisingly, not all buyers will accept the highest bid. Their agent may point out risk factors such as a buyer not being able to secure a mortgage, but a second bid came in slightly lower from an all cash buyer. What is a cash buyer you may ask? This is a buyer that has the ability to buy a home with cash and does not have to rely on a bank to borrow money to purchase a home. Even though a buyer offers a higher bid, the seller may be informed the bank will need a couple of months for the underwriter to prepare the mortgage. When bidding on a home, the seller and the seller’s agent should feel confident the offer they accept will close successfully.

Appraisals are based on comparable homes that have recently sold. The appraisers are professionals who determine the fair market value for a home based on the square footage, number of bedrooms, bathrooms, condition of the home and other factors. They determine the value added from recent remodels or architectural additions to the home. The appraiser does not take an average of the recently sold homes because each home may be slightly different. Even though condos or subdivisions have identical floor plans and square footage, the countertops and flooring could have been upgraded by the previous owners. One side of a complex could have a scenic view, which can also add value to a home.

Escrow is a neutral third party that is that keeps the funds and distributes the funds after the title is transferred. There are many financial elements that are divided between various parties on both the buying and selling side and the escrow company ensures the funds are allocated correctly. A downpayment can be submitted to the escrow company for safekeeping. It also works with the bank and insurance companies to ensure everything needed to finalize the transaction is received.

When your contracted closing date is approaching, your agent will meet with you to provide a final walk through just to make sure any necessary repairs were completed or the home was in the same condition as it was at the time your offer was accepted. Each home purchase is slightly different even though there are similar elements, so this brief summary doesn’t capture every scenario that can delay a purchase. The agents work with the clients and third parties to make all of the necessary corrections.

You will meet with the escrow or title company to sign all of the final paperwork that summarizes all of the steps, agreed upon price, payments and other details relating to the transfer of title. Congratulations! You’ve made it and you get to receive the keys to your new home.
